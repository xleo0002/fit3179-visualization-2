Mapping the Commitment to Life

In 2022, Malaysia saw a remarkable regional variation in organ donation pledges, with Selangor leading the charge, contributing to the largest share in pledges compared to the other states. This data in Selangor contrasts sharply with Labuan, where pledges were 155 times less. Such disparities underline the effectiveness of localized health campaigns and possibly the varying degrees of public health education across states.

Gender demographics revealed a nearly balanced ratio, yet with a slight male predominance in several states like Selangor and Johor, which could influence future health services planning, particularly in gender-specific health programs.

The demographic further breaksdown by age groups and ethnicity, showing youthful skew in states like Terengganu and Sabah, with over 20% of its population under the age of 25, suggesting a vibrant, growing workforce that could be harnessed for economic growth. In contrast, states like Johor and Kedah show a higher proportion of the population over the age of 65, emphasizing the need for enhanced geriatric care and services.


Educational Progress Through Time

From 2016 to 2022, Putrajaya made significant strides in improving its upper-secondary completion rates, jumping from 16th to 1st rank. Despite its population being better-educated, it lacks in donation pledge counts, suggesting no impactful relationship between education level and healthcare awareness.




A Gauge of Healthcare Infrastructure

In 2022, Kuala Lumpur had the highest ratio of hospital beds per capita in private (non-MOH) facilities, suggesting better healthcare access but also a potential over-reliance on private healthcare that may not be affordable for all. In contrast, states like Sabah, with lower bed counts, highlight urgent needs for healthcare infrastructure development.
